ORGANISE YOUR LIFE

Divide your tasks into four domains depending on their importance and urgency. Stop panicking. Take control of your life again.

The problem with lists

You know you need to keep lists. You know that if you don't keep track of the things you need to do most of them won't get done, or won't get done when they should. Too often, though, you end up doing the easy things on the list before the hard things - the low-hanging fruit. And when an important task goes uncompleted and you miss out on something good, you scream.

Why four lists is better than one.

When we categorise our tasks by their urgency and importance it becomes easy to prioritise our time, and we become calmer, more effective people. We waste less time on urgent tasks which are really not that important, and - most importantly - give time the most neglected of tasks: the ones that are important but not urgent, like cooking healthy meals, getting to the gym, making time for our loved ones, and developing our skill-sets. Presence helps you do this in the browser.
